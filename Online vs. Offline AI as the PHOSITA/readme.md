## 📌 Project Overview

This entry serves as the empirical data companion to the academic working paper published on Zenodo: **"The Sovereign Inventor: Evaluating Online vs. Offline AI as the PHOSITA Under Absolute Isolation"**. 

When an 88-day internet blockade severed connection to the global computational grid, this project was established to evaluate how localized, open-weight architectures function as a cognitive laboratory notebook to preserve the baseline of innovation. Following the restoration of connectivity, a blind diagnostic experiment was conducted to evaluate how the statutory standard of the **Person Having Ordinary Skill in the Art (PHOSITA)** manifests across differing computational environments.

### The Diagnostic Challenge
The models were cross-examined as "advanced engineering students" through a pedagogical prompting sequence. They were tasked with optimizing a high-wind, low-elevation tracking receiver array for the **Hotbird 13°E** orbital slot from a longitude of **51.5°E (Tehran, Iran)**, bounded by:
* A 1-DOF mechanical constraint along the Clarke belt.
* Passive electromagnetic metasurfaces (reflectarray antennas) to mitigate wind-shear vertical sail effects.
* Highly localized, DIY-level fabrication limits (3D-printed polymers, custom 2D Delphi rib calculations, and aluminum skin-depth boundaries).
